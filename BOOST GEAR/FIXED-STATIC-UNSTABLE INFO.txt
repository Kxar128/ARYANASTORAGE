Stable weapons play nicer with nuke skills.

For example, maybe I'm using VHL at chaos hydras. 
My 5 usually hits for around 70k. 
Suppose when it comes off cooldown, the Hydra has 20k hp left. In that case, I'll probably save the nuke for the next hydra. If the current hydra instead has 60k hp left, I'm probably best off using the nuke then. 
If I was using an unstable weapon that hit between 30k and 110k, then I'd have a lot less knowledge about whether I should use the nuke or wait, and in the 60k case, for example, I could either waste 50k damage by high rolling the nuke, or not even manage to kill the hydra with the 30k nuke. 
The lack of knowledge about what my nuke is gonna hit for hurts my ability to plan around it.

Stable weapons do better against ultra bosses. 
Suppose I hit champ drakath with that same 70k nuke. 
In the case of a stable weapon, that 70k nuke is 70k damage. However, if I were to use an unstable weapon that could nuke somewhere anywhere from 30k to 110k, the upper end of that gets reduced to only 79k, which means that I do substantially more average damage with a stable weapon.

Stable weapons do better against things with really low hp. 
For example, when I'm farming bone dust (which I usually do in a private room so I can 2 room farm), a stable weapon is gonna 1-shot the skeletons the vast majority of the time. 
On the other hand, an unstable weapon could definitely make that harder if it low rolled enough.

I've heard that there are some glitches which make certain abilities on certain classes hit harder with stable weapons
